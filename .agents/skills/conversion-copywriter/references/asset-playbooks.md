# Asset Playbooks

Use the relevant section for the asset in front of you. Do not force the same shape onto every writing task.

## Hero Section
Goal: Make the right visitor understand the offer and the next step in seconds.

Recommended structure:
- Headline: outcome or high-value promise
- Subheadline: audience + problem + mechanism
- Primary CTA: direct action
- Secondary CTA: lower-friction next step

Rules:
- Name the offer early.
- Make the headline pull specific weight.
- Keep the subheadline practical, not mystical.
- CTA labels should describe the action or result.

Checklist:
- Could a first-time visitor explain the offer after reading it?
- Is the CTA specific?
- Is the promise supported by the rest of the page?

## Product or Service Description
Goal: Help a buyer understand what it is, why it matters, and why this option is credible.

Recommended structure:
- What it is
- Why it is useful
- What changes for the buyer
- Proof or operational detail
- CTA

Rules:
- Use `FAB` when the input is feature-heavy.
- Translate internal capability into external benefit.
- Add delivery detail when proof is limited.

Checklist:
- Is the outcome clear?
- Does the copy avoid jargon?
- Is there at least one credibility signal?

## Case-Study Summary
Goal: Show transformation without sounding self-congratulatory.

Recommended structure:
- Before: context or pain
- Bridge: what was changed
- After: outcome or directional improvement
- CTA or next action

Rules:
- Use `BAB`.
- Keep the company centered only where it matters.
- If metrics are missing, use grounded operational proof instead of hype.

Checklist:
- Is the transformation visible?
- Is the proof truthful?
- Would a buyer understand relevance fast?

## Testimonial Cleanup
Goal: Make testimonial copy believable, readable, and useful.

Recommended structure:
- Who said it
- What they were struggling with
- What changed
- Why they recommend it

Rules:
- Keep original meaning intact.
- Remove filler and repeated praise.
- Do not strengthen claims beyond what the source actually supports.

Checklist:
- Does it sound like a real person?
- Is the strongest useful line easy to find?
- Did you preserve truthfulness?

## Sales Email Intro and Close
Goal: Earn a reply without sounding like bulk outreach.

Recommended structure:
- Relevance line
- Problem or missed opportunity
- Credible offer
- Low-friction CTA

Rules:
- Use `PAS` lightly.
- Keep intros short.
- Ask for one clear next step.
- Avoid fake familiarity.

Checklist:
- Is it clearly relevant to the recipient?
- Is the ask small enough?
- Could this have been sent to anyone? If yes, rewrite it.

## Ad Copy
Goal: Stop the scroll and make the offer legible immediately.

Recommended structure:
- Hook
- Pain or desire
- Offer
- Proof or differentiator
- CTA

Rules:
- Make the first line carry the weight.
- Keep each line sharp.
- Specificity usually beats intensity.

Checklist:
- Is the hook useful, not just loud?
- Is the offer obvious?
- Is the CTA concrete?

## Demo Script or Short Video Hook
Goal: Earn the next 5-30 seconds of attention.

Recommended structure:
- Hook with pain, contrast, or outcome
- State what the viewer is about to get
- Deliver the core value fast
- End with a direct CTA or takeaway

Rules:
- Write for spoken rhythm.
- Use short clauses.
- Cut any intro that delays the payoff.
- A hook should open a loop, not become clickbait.

Checklist:
- Can this be said out loud naturally?
- Does the first sentence create curiosity and clarity?
- Is the CTA aligned with the platform and format?
